## What we tried and why it failed

### Smoke #1 — 30d × top-5 × 32 configs

Result: 0/32 certified. Top SHORT +0.19R OOS but ETH-USDT ate 80% of
returns (concentration gate). Sample N too small to conclude
anything.

### Smoke #2 — 90d × top-15 × 32 configs (4h43m wall-clock)

Result: 0/32 certified. Top SHORT-c70-s2T-atr2.5: OOS +0.704R, IS
-0.178R → IS-OOS delta 125% (RED). 8 trades over 8 symbols, 1 huge
winner (AVAX +5.23R). Classic regime fit.

Important: LONG side showed max DD of 1000-2100R, which looked like
a bug. It is not — see "LONG-DD anomaly" below.

### LONG-DD anomaly (NOT a bug)

LONG-DD numbers like 1434R on a 90d window look implausible. They
are mechanically correct: 6,400 trades over 90d × 15 sym at -0.22R
each = -1,400R total → ~1,400R peak-to-trough. Verified with
`probe-long-vs-short.ts`. Same R-math as SHORT; only the trade count
differs because the bullish-classifier fires in any "above-SMA150 +
not-down-trend" regime, which is common.

### C1 — inversion experiment (`invertExecution` flag)

Hypothesis: bullish signals fire on local tops; shorting on the same
bars should win. 14d invert-probe confirmed: +0.30R raw on 5/5 syms,
N=886. **But** 30d × 32 configs × `invertExecution: true` (probe-grid-
invert): 0/32 pass, top netExp -0.10R. The 14d hit was regime-fit to a
specific bearish two-week slice. **C1 alone does not work.**

### C2 — horizon extension (36 → 144 → 288)

Mean SHORT expectancy across 32 configs:

```
h= 36 (3h)   SHORT mean -0.40R
h=144 (12h)  SHORT mean -0.13R
h=288 (24h)  SHORT mean +0.35R   ← flipped
```

Looked like a real horizon-related effect. Top SHORT-c65-s2T-atr1.5-
h288: +0.79R netExp, n=29, 26% concentration — clean-looking.

### D1 — regime check on C2 result

Same top SHORT-c65-s2T-atr1.5-h288 on two earlier 30d windows:

```
W1 (recent 30d):    +0.79R  n=29
W2 (-60 to -30d):   -0.82R  n=19   ← sign flipped
W3 (-90 to -60d):   +2.13R  n=20
```

The "edge" is a passive SHORT-bias filter that wins in bearish regimes
and loses in bullish ones. β-tracking, not α.

### Net verdict

Across 2 smokes + 5 probes + 7 distinct slices:

- LONG side: structurally -0.66 to -1.03R per trade, robust across 3 windows
- SHORT side: regime-tracking, sign flips every ~30d slice
- Inverted execution: regime-fit to a specific 14d window, does not generalize
- Horizon extension: same outcome — regime-fit
- **No tested combination of (side, threshold, stage2, atr-mult, horizon)
  produced a robust positive expectancy across multiple regimes.**

## Do not do (rabbit holes already proven dead)

- **Streaming-scoreChart side-quest.** Scoped in chat (see commits
  `a668776` / `a707432` for the diagnosis), parked because no point
  making a slower search faster when the signal does not predict.
  `precomputeScores` calls `scoreChart` with `candles.slice(0, i+1)`
  N times per (symbol, fold) — O(N²) total. Rewriting to streaming
  hoists (15 indicators × O(N) per indicator) would give ~100× speedup
  but doesn't help if the underlying signal is regime-tracking.
- **Wider smoke sweeps.** The 90d × top-15 × 32 sweep already took
  4h43m and produced 0/32. A 180d × 30 sym × 96 config sweep would
  take 5-6 days and produce 0/96 with the same diagnosis.
- **Relaxing cert gates** to "find something that passes". The gates
  are calibrated to catch regime-fits. Every hopeful result that
  passed OOS-exp failed Sharpe + IS-OOS delta. Trust the gates.
- **Re-running the inversion experiment** on a different window.
  Proven not to generalize.
- **Re-running the horizon experiment** on the same window. Proven
  not to generalize across windows.

## Critical conventions

- **ESM project**: `"type": "module"` in `package.json`. Relative
  imports MUST end in `.js` even when source is `.ts`. Get this wrong
  and tsx will fail on import.
- **One-way dep rule**: `src/analysis/*` MUST NOT import from
  `src/backtest/*`. The score-cache module (in backtest/) imports
  from analysis/, never the reverse.
- **No fold-boundaries arithmetic** — calendar-month boundaries from
  `defineFolds` (see `src/backtest/walk-forward.ts:defineFolds`). Tests
  enforce this.
- **No Date.now/Math.random** in workflow scripts (if you use the
  workflow tool — it will throw because resume requires determinism).
  Pass timestamps via args or stamp them after the run.
- **Cost model is locked** at PRD §9.3+§9.4: 0.06% taker fee per side
  + 0.01% slippage per side = 0.14% round-trip. `applyCosts` in
  `metrics.ts:applyCosts` is the single source of truth.
